◀ BACK TO THE CRAWL
A testimony, by Fede — your humble, slightly unhinged bar guide.

It started innocently enough: seven friends, one Saturday, and a wish list of drinks and snacks so specific it read like a hostage note. A pilsner here, a hazy IPA there, a Negroni, a Belgian white, a Guinness, an Old Fashioned, and somebody who just wanted chips and guac. A normal person would have picked the nearest bar and called it a day. I am not a normal person.

So I did what any reasonable maniac would do: I summoned a council of robots. I commissioned full research reports from Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — and naturally they all disagreed. One marched everyone a mile south to Morgan Junction. One confidently recommended bars that may or may not exist. One tried to make us watch Ecuador–Curaçao in three places at once. Each was certain it had cracked it. I cross-examined them like a tired detective.

Then came the real work: cross-referencing menus, local blogs, Reddit threads, neighborhood history, World Cup kickoff times, reservation policies, and walking distances measured down to the block. Chains-from-elsewhere were eliminated on sight (a moment of silence for the Matador). We caught Lady Jaye's secret pivot to Wagyu smash burgers. We confirmed Husky Deli would still be open. Every survivor had to be a genuine West Seattle original and earn its place against the wish list — no filler, no tourist traps.

The verdict: four originals plus an epilogue, north Admiral down to the Junction — soccer at the start, beer in the middle, the best burger in town, and s'mores by a fake campfire to close. Then I built a Game Boy app for it, because if you're going to over-plan a night out, you may as well do it in 16-bit. This is the product of genuine love for my friends and a frankly alarming number of tokens. Drink responsibly. Tip your guide.

— Fede 🍺⚓
Chief Crawl Officer, West Seattle Division
